The route

Two hours in the heart
of an ancient republic

We stay close to Piazza De Ferrari, exploring the palaces, alleys, and sacred spaces that made Genova one of the most powerful cities in Europe.

I

Piazza De Ferrari

Our meeting point — Genova's grand symbolic centre, dominated by the iconic bronze fountain and surrounded by neoclassical façades. We begin with the city's modern identity before diving into its ancient soul.

II

Palazzo Ducale

Right on the square, the palace of the Doges — rulers of the Genoese Republic for centuries. We uncover the political intrigues, the prisoners, and the art hidden inside its grand halls.

III

Cattedrale di San Lorenzo

Just steps away — the striking black-and-white striped Gothic cathedral consecrated in 1118. Home to relics, cannonballs, and legends that span nearly a thousand years.

IV

The Caruggi — ancient alleys

We plunge into the medieval labyrinth of narrow lanes — Europe's largest medieval historic centre. Here tradespeople, sailors, and nobles once lived side by side in a city bursting with commerce.

V

Piazza Matteotti & Teatro Carlo Felice

Adjacent to De Ferrari, Genova's opera house and civic square complete the grand neoclassical ensemble of the city centre. We finish here with a view of the whole picture — past and present.

FAQ

Good to know

Where exactly do we meet?

At the central fountain in Piazza De Ferrari — the large bronze fountain right in the middle of the square. Look for your guide holding a "Walk Genova" sign. Please arrive 5–10 minutes early.

When does the tour run?

Daily at 10:00 AM and 3:00 PM. We run in all weather, so dress accordingly. Tours may be cancelled in exceptional circumstances — we'll always notify you in advance if you've booked.

Do I need to book in advance?

Booking is strongly recommended so we can manage group size and confirm your spot. Use the form below or send us a WhatsApp — it takes less than a minute.

What should I wear?

Comfortable walking shoes are essential — Genova's cobblestones are beautiful but uneven. Bring water in summer. A light layer in winter months goes a long way.

Is the tour accessible?

The route includes some steps and narrow alleys typical of the old city. If you have specific accessibility needs, please contact us before booking and we'll do our best to accommodate you.

Can children join?

Absolutely — children are welcome and usually love the stories about pirates, merchants, and medieval life. Under 12s join free (no tip expected).
